Predicting churn before it happens

Cloudways (DigitalOcean) · 2023–2025
Problem

The growth team could see accounts churning but had no way to flag who was at risk before they left — every retention effort was reactive.

Method

Built a logistic regression churn-prediction model in R on SQL-extracted product event and behavioral data. Used cohort analysis to isolate drop-off patterns and identify which segments the model should weight most.

Outcome

Proposed and drove a retention initiative directly off the model's output, rather than handing findings to another team to interpret.

+10%retention over 3 quarters
R + SQLlogistic regression model
100M+ARR platform

Researching adoption of an AI Copilot feature

Cloudways (DigitalOcean) · 2024

Method

Testing what actually moves upgrade behavior

Cloudways (DigitalOcean) · 2023–2025
Problem

Product, pricing, and UX changes were being proposed on instinct, with no consistent way to tell which ones would actually move adoption or upgrade behavior.

Method

Designed and ran A/B tests alongside large-scale surveys (n=300–2,000), applying statistical significance testing to separate real signal from noise.

Outcome

Pushed teams toward one specific next action per test, instead of leaving them with open-ended findings to debate.

Mapping a service blueprint for the entire org

Cloudways (DigitalOcean) · 2023–2025
Problem

As a founder-led startup, Cloudways had grown into working silos — departments were often unaware of what other teams were doing on the same customer issues, leading to repetitive work and slow resolution at the most basic level.

Method

Ran stakeholder interviews across departments and facilitated journey mapping workshops to reconstruct how work actually moved through the org, rather than how each team assumed it moved.

Outcome

The blueprint became the single source of truth for onboarding new employees, and gave org leaders a shared reference to spot loopholes and work toward breaking down the silos it had surfaced.
